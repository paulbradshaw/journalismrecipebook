# How to write... an anniversary feature

News organisations regularly use anniversaries as a news 'hook' to hang a story on and plan around. Here are some examples:

* [One year on: how landmark ruling on single-sex spaces has changed lives](https://www.theguardian.com/society/2026/apr/15/how-scotland-landmark-ruling-single-sex-spaces-changed-lives) uses the one year anniversary of a supreme court judgment
* In politics and war, 100 days is often used as a key moment to review what has happened, as in [Never mind the flag bans, where are the policies? Assessing 100 days of Reform-led councils](https://www.theguardian.com/politics/ng-interactive/2025/aug/30/100-days-of-reform-led-councils-flag-bans-policies) and [100 days of war in Ukraine: The devastating consequences of Putin's invasion](https://news.sky.com/story/100-days-of-war-in-ukraine-the-devastating-consequences-of-putins-invasion-12626510)
* A decade on a major sporting event is a good opportunity to look back at its legacy: [‘A Jerusalem for everyone’: was the 2012 Olympics the last gasp of liberal Britain?](https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2022/jul/16/danny-boyle-2012-london-olympic-opening-ceremony-feelgood-factor?CMP=insidesaturday_email)
* Fashion editor Jess Cartner-Morley uses an anniversary to explore why one shopping mall is bucking the trend: [Bicester Village at 30: how a wet field in Oxfordshire defied the death of the shopping mall](https://www.theguardian.com/fashion/2025/may/10/bicester-village-30-oxfordshire-shopping-mall)
* Even 35 years can be a round enough number, as in [Miners' strike: The decades-old feud that still divides communities](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-nottinghamshire-47401859)
* 100 years is a big anniversary. Stories like [Spanish flu: 'We didn't know who we'd lose next'](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-45097068) rely on interviews with experts, documents and data

Anniversary stories are most often written as features - but it's worth mentioning that they can also act as a hook for a news story (e.g. "A year on from a new law, new data reveals no one has been charged") and liveblogs ([EFL Rewind: Relive the opening day of Football League action 130 years after it happened](https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/live/football/45201526)).
