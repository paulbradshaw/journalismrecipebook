# How to: write a story about a new report

*[Iran war driving up funeral costs in the UK](https://www.theguardian.com/money/2026/apr/05/funeral-costs-uk-iran-war)* by Rupert Jones.


## Identify the 'top line' as use that for the headline and first paragraph

Look at the report and decide what's the most newsworthy thing that's been said. Your headline is likely to be quote-driven, e.g. '"X", says report" or 'New report: "X"'.

In this case it's *New UK farm inheritance tax rule will cause ‘significant challenges’, say accountants*. 

The first line proper (excluding any 'second headline' or standfirst) of the story should repeat this, as the story does here:

> *A new inheritance tax regime for UK farms and family businesses comes into force on Monday and will present “significant challenges” for those affected, according to accountants.*

## Add context to explain why it's important

The second par of any news story tends to flesh out the first par with further details on the 5Ws (who, when, where, what, how, why) and any key context. Sometimes, however, there may be more background to establish first. That's the case here, with four paragraphs of background before the story continues explaining who has said what.

> *In October 2024 the government announced plans to levy inheritance tax on farms – prompting an outcry in many quarters.*

## Explain who/what in more detail

After those four pars, then, we dive into the detail of the story: the specific name of the company producing the report, and the name of a person from that company:

> *Elsa Littlewood, a private client partner at accountancy and business advisory firm BDO, said the start of the new inheritance tax regime was “a watershed moment for the farming and family business community”.*

This continues for a further four pars before the final par of the story.

## Weave in some response

Once we get the key point(s) from the report, there needs to be some response to it - or at least an indication that an opportunity has been provided to give one (a no comment). 

In this case, the response comes from previous reporting:

> *The government previously said it had listened to the concerns raised and that raising the threshold would significantly cut the number of farms and business owners facing higher inheritance tax bills, ensuring that only the largest estates were affected.*

## End by looking ahead to what comes next

That is actually the final par here - but often the last par of a news story generally adds something that is happening next.
